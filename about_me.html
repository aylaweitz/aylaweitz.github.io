Hi, I'm Ayla!

I'm a California bay-area native who recently graduated from UC Berkeley with a B.A. in Astrophysics and is currently a Research Associate at Lockheed Martin Solar and Astrophysics Laboratory. I’m interested space weather forecasting, coronal heating, and Type Ia supernovae. While my research interests range from astrophysical phenomena near and far, my underlying passion lies in working with data and developing software to tackle interesting problems.

The work I've done includes improving a space weather forecasting software, refining Type Ia supernovae coordinates, and developing methods for standardizing Type Ia supernovae spectra.

When I'm not coding or thinking about space, you'll probably find me backpacking, cooking vegetarian noodle stir frys, napping with my cat, making pomegranate juice, or tending to my plants.
